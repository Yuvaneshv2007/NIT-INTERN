$- Indications for adjuvant post-op radiotherapy are T3-T4 primary, Node positivity, perineural invasion, lympho-vascular invasion, and poorly differentiated disease. IMRT may be considered (c) if affordable (employer insurance scheme, personal insurance schemes) and

@- Borderline Resectable - This is broadly a situation wherein the primary tumor is grossly
resectable, but significant concern exists regarding the probability of a positive resection
margin or excessive surgical morbidity. The decision regarding borderline resectablity should
be taken by a surgeon (preferably in a multidisciplinary tumor board). Situations which may
be deemed as borderline resectable are1. Soft tissue swelling up to the zygoma in case of a BM-GBS primary.
2. Disease close to hyoid or valleculae in case of a Tongue primary.
3. Some situations with Extensive skin infiltration and Involvement of

(Supra-notch) infratemporal fossa.
Radiological involvement of the infratemporal fossa which is inferior to the level of the mandibular notch is deemed as resectable by current surgical techniques.

offered as per the algorithm for operable oral cancers.
